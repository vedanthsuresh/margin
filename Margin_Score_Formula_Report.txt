                    MARGIN SCORE CALCULATION FORMULA

Version: 1.0.0
Date: July 15, 2026
Component: Margin Score Engine

================================================================================
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

The Margin Score is a 0-100 metric representing a user's real-time emotional 
and mental capacity. It is calculated using a deterministic formula that 
combines self-reported inputs with contextual enrichment factors.

================================================================================
HOW IT WORKS: THE BIG PICTURE
================================================================================

Think of the Margin Score like a weather forecast for your mental bandwidth. 
Just as a weather report combines temperature, humidity, and wind to give you 
a "feels like" temperature, the Margin Score combines multiple factors to give 
you a "feels like" capacity reading.

The calculation happens in two stages:

  1. BASE SCORE: Calculated from your direct inputs
  2. ENRICHED SCORE: Base score adjusted by contextual factors

================================================================================
THE FORMULA
================================================================================

STEP 1: CALCULATE BASE SCORE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The base score comes from three user inputs:

+-------------+-------------+----------+--------------------------+
| Input       | Range       | Weight   | Impact on Score          |
+-------------+-------------+----------+--------------------------+
| Sleep Hours | 0-10        | 30%      | More sleep = higher score |
| Energy Level| 1-10        | 40%      | Higher energy = higher   |
| Meeting Load| 0-16        | 30%      | More meetings = lower     |
+-------------+-------------+----------+--------------------------+

Base Score Formula:

  base_score = (sleep_normalized × 0.30) + (energy × 0.40) - (meeting_load × 0.30)

Where sleep_normalized converts 0-10 hours to a 0-100 scale (7-8 hours = optimal).

STEP 2: APPLY CONTEXTUAL ADJUSTMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The base score is then adjusted by contextual factors:

  enriched_score = base_score
                    + day_factor
                    + seasonal_factor
                    + holiday_bonus (if applicable)
                    - timezone_penalty (if applicable)
                    - company_size_adjustment
                    ± industry_comparison_delta
                    ± sleep_impact_adjustment
                    ± stress_adjustments
                    ± work_life_penalties

================================================================================
EACH FACTOR EXPLAINED
================================================================================

1. DAY FACTOR
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Adjusts score based on day of week:

+-----------+------------------------------+
| Day       | Adjustment                   |
+-----------+------------------------------+
| Monday    | -3 (week catch-up fatigue)   |
| Tuesday   | 0 (baseline)                 |
| Wednesday | +2 (mid-week peak)           |
| Thursday  | 0 (baseline)                 |
| Friday    | -2 (week-end fatigue)        |
| Saturday  | +5 (weekend buffer)          |
| Sunday    | +3 (weekend preparation)     |
+-----------+------------------------------+

Example: A base score of 70 on Monday becomes 67. On Wednesday, it becomes 72.

2. SEASONAL FACTOR
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Adjusts score based on quarter of year:

+----------------+------------------------------+
| Quarter        | Adjustment                   |
+----------------+------------------------------+
| Q1 (Jan-Mar)   | -2 (post-holiday recovery)  |
| Q2 (Apr-Jun)   | +2 (spring momentum)        |
| Q3 (Jul-Sep)   | 0 (summer steady state)     |
| Q4 (Oct-Dec)   | -5 (year-end crunch)        |
+----------------+------------------------------+

3. HOLIDAY BONUS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
US Federal holidays provide a capacity boost. The system includes 2024 and 2025 
holidays with "buffer days" - days immediately before/after the holiday when 
the adjustment also applies.

Major holiday adjustments:
  • Thanksgiving:     +10 (2 buffer days)
  • Christmas/New Year: +8 (1-2 buffer days)
  • Independence Day:  +6 (1 buffer day)
  • Memorial Day:     +5 (1 buffer day)
  • Labor Day:        +4 (1 buffer day)

4. TIMEZONE PENALTY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Working across timezones reduces capacity:

+-------------------------------+----------+
| Timezone Span                 | Penalty  |
+-------------------------------+----------+
| Single timezone               | 0        |
| Dual (US coasts)              | -3       |
| Multi (3+ zones)              | -7       |
| Global (APAC/EMEA/Americas)   | -10      |
+-------------------------------+----------+

5. COMPANY SIZE ADJUSTMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Organization size affects meeting load and bureaucracy:

+-------------------------+----------+
| Company Size            | Adjustment|
+-------------------------+----------+
| Startup                 | -3       |
| Small (10-50)           | -2       |
| Mid-market (50-500)     | 0        |
| Enterprise (500+)       | -2       |
| Mega corp (10K+)        | -4       |
+-------------------------+----------+

6. INDUSTRY COMPARISON DELTA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Your meeting load is compared to industry averages for your role. If you're 
above average, your score decreases.

Example roles included:
  • Software Engineer: 6 avg meeting hours
  • Product Manager: 12 avg meeting hours
  • Executive: 15 avg meeting hours
  • Freelancer: 4 avg meeting hours

Formula: delta = your_meeting_hours - industry_average_hours

7. SLEEP IMPACT ADJUSTMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sleep duration directly affects performance:

+---------------+------------------------------+
| Hours Slept   | Adjustment                   |
+---------------+------------------------------+
| 7-9 hours     | +5 (optimal)                 |
| 6 or 10 hours | +2 (adequate)                |
| 5 or 11 hours | -3 (deficit)                 |
| 0-4 or 12+    | -10 (critical)               |
+---------------+------------------------------+

8. STRESS INDICATORS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Meeting patterns affect cognitive load:

Stress factors applied:
  • Back-to-back meetings (>3): alert triggered
  • Context switching penalty: -2 per back-to-back meeting
  • No lunch break: -5 penalty
  • Well-distributed meetings: +2 bonus

9. WORK-LIFE BALANCE PENALTIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Boundary violations significantly reduce capacity:

+----------------------------+----------+
| Violation                  | Penalty  |
+----------------------------+----------+
| After-hours communication  | -3       |
| Weekend work               | -5       |
| Late meeting (after 6pm)   | -2       |
| Late meeting (after 8pm)   | -4       |
| Early meeting (before 8am)| -2       |
| Early meeting (before 7am)| -4       |
+----------------------------+----------+

================================================================================
COMPLETE CALCULATION EXAMPLE
================================================================================

User: Sarah, Software Engineer
Date: Monday, November 18, 2024 (Q4, pre-Thanksgiving)

STEP 1: USER INPUTS
  • Sleep: 6 hours
  • Energy: 7/10
  • Meeting Load: 10 hours

STEP 2: CALCULATE BASE SCORE
  base = (60 × 0.30) + (70 × 0.40) - (62.5 × 0.30)
  base = 18 + 28 - 18.75
  base = 27.25

STEP 3: APPLY ADJUSTMENTS
  adjustments = day_factor (-3) + seasonal_factor (-5) 
                + sleep_impact (+2) - industry_delta (-4)
  adjustments = -3 - 5 + 2 - 4 = -10

STEP 4: FINAL SCORE
  final_score = 27.25 + (-10)
  final_score = 17.25
  final_score = 17 (rounded)

Sarah's Margin Score: 17% — Depleted Capacity
Color Zone: Red/Orange
Response Mode: Friction Lock activated

================================================================================
TECHNICAL NOTES
================================================================================

DETERMINISTIC DESIGN:
  • The formula is entirely deterministic — same inputs always produce the same score
  • No AI or machine learning is used in the calculation
  • All contextual data comes from static JSON files served by the backend
  • The calculation runs locally on the device (Flutter app)
  • Only the AI response generation uses an LLM (Vertex AI/Gemini)

WHY THIS DESIGN?
  • Speed: Instant calculation without API latency
  • Cost: No LLM token costs for score updates
  • Predictability: Transparent, testable logic
  • Offline capability: Works without internet
